The Big Chop

On Wednesday, I scheduled my first haircut in nearly 5 years! I started growing my hair out in January of 2016, with the intention of one day having a “man bun”. By the Fall of 2016, my hair was finally long enough to go up in a bun… and from there I just stuck with the status quo. I didn’t really have much of a plan, so my hair just kept growing and growing!

Over the last few months, I had started to get a little tired of it’s extreme length. It had gotten to the point where it was almost too long for a bun and, after showering, my hair would remain wet for hours! This month, I decided that I was going to go ahead and commit, so I scheduled the big chop.

It has definitely been a significant change! I keep running my hands through my hair, and I admit to surprising myself a few times when I walk by a mirror. So far, I haven’t really missed the length though — I think I’ve been so busy enjoying not having hair in my eyes, that I haven’t really felt any remorse for the missing locks.

Fall Festivities

On Saturday, Anna and I went hiking in Berea. We started off by getting some bagels at Native Bagel Co — The salt bagel with honey rosemary cream cheese is amazing! We then hiked up to the top of East Pinnacle. Even though we were hoping for more Fall colors, the view was amazing and the weather was perfect for a nice hike. Afterwards, we drove over to Eckert’s Orchard for some more Fall fun! We ordered their warm doughnut Sunday: a warm apple cider doughnut, covered with vanilla ice cream, and drizzled with caramel syrup. It was sooooo good!
